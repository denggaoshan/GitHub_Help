Redeeming a coupon for your personal account

  • mac
  • windows
  • linux
  • all

With a coupon code, you can apply a discounted (or free!) private plan to your account.

There are two types of coupons available:

  • Plan-specific coupons can only be applied to specific accounts. For example, if the coupon grants a Bronze plan, it can only be applied to an organization.
  • Discount coupons are not tied to any specific plan or account. For example, a $25 off coupon can be applied to your personal account (which would get you a free Medium user plan), or an organization (which would give you a free Bronze plan). If you use a discount coupon on a Gold plan, you would end up paying $75 instead of the normal $100.

Applying the coupon

Warning: GitHub can't issue a refund if you pay for an account before applying a coupon. We also can't transfer a redeemed coupon or give you a new coupon if you apply it to the wrong account. Be certain you are applying the coupon to the correct account before you proceed.

  1. Visit the Redeem Coupon page.
  2. In the Enter Coupon Code box, type your coupon code and click Redeem.
  3. If you're not already signed in, you can sign in on this page, or create a new account to apply your coupon to.
  4. On the Redeem Coupon page, click Choose next to the personal account you want to apply the coupon to.
  5. Under Choose your plan, verify the type of plan you want to apply the coupon to. Your current plan is already selected, but if you have a discount coupon, you can choose to upgrade your plan and apply the discount to that new upgrade. After you've verified the correct plan type, click Redeem.
