How does upgrading or downgrading affect the billing process?

  • mac
  • windows
  • linux
  • all

Changing your account's plan, or the plan of an organization you administer, affects the billing cycle is various ways.

The effects of changing your plan, both on the number of private repositories you are granted, as well as how you are billed, are the same for both personal accounts and organizations you administer.

What happens to my account when I change my plan?

When you upgrade or downgrade your account, the new plan is immediately applied to your account. In other words, if you're upgrading, you get instant access to more private repositories. If you're downgrading to a smaller (or free), the private repositories you have are instantly locked.

What happens to my billing when I change my plan?

The effect on how much you're billed depends on whether you're on a monthly or yearly billing cycle.

Monthly billing

Monthly users are billed at the new plan rate on their next monthly billing date. Since we don't count partial months of the new plan, you may get some complimentary days of upgraded access while only having to pay the lower price.

Example: Eric is a monthly user and pays for the Small Plan on the 15th of every month. If, on July 31st, Eric upgrades from the Small to the Medium Plan, he get the benefits of the Medium Plan immediately, but only starts paying for the new Medium Plan rate on August 15th.

For downgrades, the converse is true:

Example: Rebecca is a monthly user and pays for the Small Plan on the 15th of every month. If, on July 31st, Rebecca downgrades from the Small to the Micro Plan, her account immediately switches to the Micro Plan, but Rebecca won't be refunded for the period from August 1st to 15th for which she has already paid the cost of the Small Plan.

This policy applies even if you are downgrading to a free account:

Example: Joe is a monthly user and pays for the Micro Plan on the 5th of every month. If, on October 10th, Joe downgrades from the Small to the Free Plan, his account immediately switches to the Free Plan, access to his private repositories are locked, and Joe won't be refunded for the remaining unused portion of the month.

Yearly billing

Yearly users are only billed for the difference in price between their old and new rates based on the number of unused whole months. Again, since we don't count partial months of the new plan, you may get some complimentary days of upgraded access while having paid the lower price.

Example: Mada is a yearly user and usually pays for the Small Plan on July 15th of every year. If, on July 31st, she upgrades from the Small to the Medium Plan, she immediately gets the benefits of the Medium Plan. She is also billed immediately for the difference between Small and Medium plans from the next remaining whole month to the end of her current yearly billing cycle. She pays the extra amount it costs for Medium from August 15th through July 15th of the following year, even though she had 11.5 months on the Medium Plan.

For downgrades, yearly users are refunded the difference in price between their old and new rates based on the number of unused and partially used whole months. Again, you may get some complimentary days of upgraded access while having paid the lower price.

Example: Mada is a yearly user and usually pays for the Small Plan on July 15th of every year. If, on July 31st, Mada decides to downgrade from the Small to the Micro Plan, her account immediately switches to the Micro Plan, and she is refunded the difference between Small and Micro plans for 12 months (until July 15th of the following year).

This policy applies even if you are downgrading to a free account.

Example: Joe is a yearly user and usually pays for the Micro Plan on October 5th every year. If, on October 10th, Joe downgrades from the Small to the Free Plan, his account immediately switches to the Free Plan, access to his private repositories will be locked, and Joe is refunded for the entire month of October, and the remaining unused months in his yearly billing cycle, which would be a total of 12 months.
