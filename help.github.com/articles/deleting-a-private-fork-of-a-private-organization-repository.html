Deleting a private fork of a private organization repository

  • mac
  • windows
  • linux
  • all

If a member leaves your organization, you may need to delete their private fork of one of your organization's private repositories.

Warning: You can't delete public forks of a repository that was public when it was forked, even if the repository is now private. For more information, see What happens to forks when a repository is deleted or changes visibility?

Only members of the Owners team can delete forks of an organization's repositories.

  1. In the top right corner of any page, click your username.

  2. On the left side of your profile page, under "Organizations", click the icon for your organization.

  3. Click the name of the repository.

  4. In the repository sidebar, click Graphs.

  5. Above the Contributors graph, click the Members tab.
  6. In the list of forks, find the user whose fork you want to delete. Click the name of the forked repository.
  7. Follow the instructions for Deleting a repository.
